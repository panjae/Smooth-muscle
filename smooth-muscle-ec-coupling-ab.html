Chapter 13. III. EXCITATION-CONTRACTION COUPLING

A. Calcium Source & B. Contraction

The number of competing signals that vie for control of smooth muscle function is immense (hundreds). Most converge on Ca²⁺, just as in other muscle types. The main difference between smooth and skeletal muscle is that the Ca²⁺-sensitive step has been transferred from the thin filament (via troponin) to the thick filament (via myosin phosphorylation).

Contraction begins when sarcoplasmic [Ca²⁺] rises from 0.1 μM (rest).

B. Contraction

Unlike striated muscle where troponin moves tropomyosin, smooth muscle requires myosin phosphorylation.

Key Steps

  • Ca²⁺ binds to Calmodulin (CaM)
    4 Ca²⁺ per CaM
    Conformational change
  • Ca²⁺-CaM activates MLCK
    Myosin light-chain kinase
    Serine/threonine kinase
    Ca²⁺-dependent activation
  • MLCK phosphorylates MLC20
    20-kDa regulatory light chain
    Serine-19 phosphorylation
    Enables ATPase activity
  • Phosphorylated myosin binds actin
  • Crossbridge cycling proceeds
  • ATP powers contraction

Key Differences

  • Striated: High intrinsic ATPase
  • Smooth: ATPase inactive until phosphorylated
  • Allows precise control
  • Energy conservation

📋 Clinical Application 13.1: Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

IBS is a GI disorder with intestinal cramping, flatulence, and altered bowel habits of unknown cause. Treatment includes antispasmodics like L-hyoscyamine (atropine analog) - a cholinergic receptor antagonist that blocks parasympathetic-induced smooth muscle contractility. Natural remedies include peppermint oil, which relaxes smooth muscle. However, it's contraindicated in GERD patients as it also relaxes the lower esophageal sphincter (LES), exacerbating symptoms.

💡 Core Concept: Ca²⁺ Sensitivity Transfer

The fundamental difference between smooth and skeletal muscle lies in where Ca²⁺ sensitivity resides. Skeletal muscle uses Ca²⁺-troponin interaction on the thin filament to expose myosin-binding sites on actin. Smooth muscle transfers this Ca²⁺ sensitivity to the thick filament through the Ca²⁺-CaM-MLCK pathway, requiring myosin phosphorylation for contraction. This allows for more diverse regulation through multiple signaling pathways that converge on MLCK/MLCP balance rather than simple Ca²⁺ binding.
